# Glossary (Plain Language)

Status: working draft

## 1. Purpose

This glossary standardizes day-to-day terms used across the Work Delivery Framework and its related specifications.

Use it to reduce re-orientation time, avoid near-synonyms that hide control meaning, and keep discussions consistent across stages.

## 2. How to Use This Glossary

- If a term appears in a framework/process document and feels ambiguous, check it here first.
- Prefer the definitions here when writing or reviewing governed work artifacts.
- If a term is still unclear after this glossary, follow the "See also" link to the controlling document.

See also:
- [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)
- [Deliverable Specifications Index](deliverable_specifications_index.md)
- [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md)

## 3. Glossary

### Acceptance

Formal confirmation by the named **Acceptance Authority** that an in-scope deliverable (or deliverable domain) meets the defined acceptance focus and has sufficient evidence.

See also: [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)

### Acceptance Authority

The person accountable for accepting deliverables within an in-scope deliverable domain (or a defined set of deliverables).

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md) (see Section 17 - Role Glossary)

### Acceptance Evidence

The reviewable proof that supports acceptance (for example: test results, approvals, screenshots, logs, training completion, migration verification, monitoring checks).

Practical rule: collect it as you deliver; do not reconstruct it at the end.

See also: [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)

### Acceptance Focus

The specific "done means..." expectations for a deliverable or deliverable domain (what will be checked at acceptance).

### Artifact

Any written record produced during delivery (drafts, notes, forms, decisions). Not all artifacts are deliverables.

Related: **Deliverable**

### Baseline (Definition Baseline)

The agreed definition of the work (scope boundary, intended outcomes, domains/deliverables, owners, major commitments). After Stage 3, this becomes the authorized baseline unless formally changed.

Related: **Scope Boundary**, **Change Control**

### Change Control

The rules and records used to make scope changes explicit, attributable, and reviewable (instead of silently expanding work).

Related: **Decision Record**, **Material Change**

### Closure

The formal end of the work item, recorded with an explicit closure basis (accepted / conditionally accepted / closed with follow-up obligations) and ownership transitioned as needed.

See also: [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)

### Conditional Acceptance

Acceptance given with explicit conditions that must still be completed, owned, and tracked.

Practical rule: conditions must be visible, attributable, and time-bounded.

### Decision Authority

The person accountable for the go / no-go decision to commit organizational capacity, time, funding, and risk.

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md) (see Section 17 - Role Glossary)

### Decision-Ready

A Stage 2 definition state where the work is clear enough to decide on (scope boundary, outcomes, ownership, deliverable domains, major risks/constraints), without becoming detailed design.

Related: **Delivery-Ready**

### Decision Record

A documented decision with: decision statement, decision maker, date, rationale, options considered (as needed), and implications (scope/cost/time/risk).

See also: [Deliverable Specifications Index](deliverable_specifications_index.md)

### Define-Decide-Deliver (DDD)

The work management logic used in this repository:
- **Define** what the work is and why it matters
- **Decide** whether to commit capacity, funding, and risk
- **Deliver** within the agreed controls, then accept and close

See also: [ITS Work Management Model](../its_work_management_model.md)

### Deliverable

An output that is required (or explicitly agreed) for the work item and is eligible for acceptance (for example: Initiative Definition Document, Functional Capabilities, Security Risk Assessment, Operations & Support Model).

Related: **Deliverable Domain**, **Acceptance**

### Deliverable Domain

A grouping used to organize deliverables and acceptance ownership (for example: Governance & Control, Solution Deliverables, Operational Readiness).

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md)

### Delivery Mobilization

Stage 5 work that sets up the operating rhythm and controls needed to execute: trackers, cadence, reporting, escalation path, change path, and acceptance-readiness tracking.

See also: [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)

### Delivery Owner

The person accountable for stage discipline, coordination, delivery control, and keeping required deliverables and evidence ready for acceptance.

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md) (see Section 17 - Role Glossary)

### Delivery-Ready

A Stage 4 state where the authorized baseline has been elaborated enough to build/test/transition without inventing scope during execution.

Related: **Decision-Ready**

### Evidence (Delivery Evidence)

The linked records that demonstrate what was delivered, tested, reviewed, and approved. Evidence must be attributable and reviewable.

Related: **Acceptance Evidence**, **Traceability**

### Functional Capability (FC-###)

A stable ID and statement of something the solution must do (used to keep scope and traceability stable across design, build, test, and acceptance).

See also: [Solution Design Process](solution_design_process.md)

### Governance (in this repository)

The minimum controls that keep planned work understandable, controllable, supportable, and formally closable: clear scope, named owners, decision rights, deliverables, evidence, acceptance, and closure.

See also: [ITS Operating Model](../its_operating_model.md)

### Handover / Transition

The explicit transfer of responsibility to the receiving owner(s) (often operations/support), including confirmation that the work is supportable beyond the delivery team.

Related: **Operational Readiness**, **Closure**

### Initiative Definition Document (IDD)

The Stage 2 definition baseline for an initiative: why the work matters, scope boundary, outcomes, domains/deliverables, ownership, and decision-ready commitments.

See also: [Deliverable Specifications Index](deliverable_specifications_index.md)

### Material Change

A change that alters outcomes, acceptance expectations, budget, schedule, risk, or authorized scope boundary. Material changes must be explicitly decided and recorded.

Related: **Change Control**, **Baseline**

### Non-Negotiables (Controls)

The minimum requirements that should not be skipped even when artifacts are kept light: scope boundary, named owners, decision rights, acceptance mapping, evidence, and explicit closure.

See also: [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)

### Outcome Owner

The person accountable for the intended business outcomes and confirming that delivered value meets the agreed success measures.

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md) (see Section 17 - Role Glossary)

### Operational Readiness

The set of deliverables and checks needed to ensure the solution is supportable in real operation (support model, monitoring, backup/recovery intent, runbooks, ownership).

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md)

### RAID Log

A working record of **R**isks, **A**ssumptions, **I**ssues, and **D**ependencies used to keep delivery visible and governable during execution.

### Scope Boundary

The explicit "in scope / out of scope" boundary. It is required early (working boundary) and becomes controlled after authorization (authorized baseline).

### Sponsor

The person who confirms why the work matters, commits support (and often funding/priority), and accepts organizational risk.

See also: [Standard Deliverables Guide](standard_deliverables_guide.md) (see Section 17 - Role Glossary)

### Solution Module (SM-###)

A stable ID for a cohesive part of the solution (a module/component) used to organize design, build, and evidence without changing scope.

See also: [Solution Design Process](solution_design_process.md)

### Stage (Stage 1-7)

The governed path used for planned work:
1. Work Assessment
2. Work Definition
3. Work Authorization
4. Work Definition Details
5. Delivery Mobilization
6. Work Delivery
7. Acceptance, Transition & Closure

See also: [Work Delivery Framework](work_delivery_framework.md)

### Traceability

The ability to follow a line from assessment -> definition -> capabilities/deliverables -> evidence -> acceptance/closure, so decisions and outcomes remain explainable and supportable.

See also: [Solution Design Process](solution_design_process.md)

### Use Case Narrative (UC-###)

A stable ID and narrative that describes how a user/actor achieves a goal with the solution, used for design, testing, and acceptance evidence.

See also: [Solution Design Process](solution_design_process.md)

### Work Brief

A smaller governed work definition record used when the work is intentionally scoped as one controllable item (instead of a full initiative document set).

See also: [Deliverable Specifications Index](deliverable_specifications_index.md)

### Working Draft

A state label used when control inputs are missing/unclear (stage, scope boundary, owners, decision basis, evidence basis, acceptance authority). It means "review needed before treating as authoritative".
