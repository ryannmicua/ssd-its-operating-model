# Delivery Owner Runbook

Status: working draft

## Purpose

This runbook is the day-to-day control dashboard for Delivery Owners running planned ITS work.

Use it to reduce re-orientation during delivery by keeping the repeat-use checks, links, and stage decisions in one place.

## Start Here

Use this document for:

- checking what must happen next at the current stage
- running the minimum weekly operating rhythm
- spotting control failures early before they become acceptance or closure problems

Do not use this document for:

- replacing the controlling framework or specifications
- drafting detailed deliverables instead of opening the correct specification

## Use This When

- you are the named Delivery Owner, acting Delivery Owner, or project manager working on the Delivery Owner's behalf
- the work is planned ITS work that needs visible scope, decisions, evidence, and acceptance
- you need a stable navigation point while work is in motion

## What You Need First

Before using this runbook, confirm:

- the current stage
- the current scope boundary
- the Decision Authority
- the named Acceptance Authorities for the deliverables or domains in scope
- the primary work record in use (`Work Brief` or `Initiative Definition Document`)

## What To Do

1. Identify the current stage and its exit signal.
2. Confirm the minimum outputs and the required decision at that stage.
3. Keep decisions, changes, evidence, and acceptance readiness current while work is moving.
4. Use the linked specification for the exact artifact you need.
5. Do not treat delivery as complete until acceptance and closure are explicit.

## Stage Checklist

| Stage | What you are trying to achieve | Minimum outputs to keep visible | Required decision | Exit signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Stage 1 - Work Assessment | Decide whether definition is justified | Initial Review, Validation Assessment, Work Assessment Report as needed | Continue, defer, or reject | Stage 1 recommendation is explicit and recorded |
| Stage 2 - Work Definition | Create a decision-ready baseline | `Work Brief` or `Initiative Definition Document`, scope boundary, owners, deliverables, Acceptance Authorities | Ready or not ready for authorization | Definition is decision-ready, not design-heavy |
| Stage 3 - Work Authorization | Get explicit approval to proceed | authorization decision record, conditions, major decision basis | Authorize, defer, or reject | explicit authorization is recorded |
| Stage 4 - Work Definition Details | Elaborate only what was authorized | delivery-ready detail, solution and operational artifacts, acceptance focus, evidence expectations | Continue as authorized or raise change | detail is ready without silent scope expansion |
| Stage 5 - Delivery Mobilization | Start controlled execution | cadence, trackers, delivery plan, RAID view, acceptance tracker | Ready to mobilize or not ready | the work can run without hiding readiness gaps |
| Stage 6 - Work Delivery | Deliver outputs and gather evidence | deliverables, evidence links, status, RAID updates, change records | Continue, escalate, or change | in-scope outputs are delivered and ready for acceptance review |
| Stage 7 - Acceptance, Transition & Closure | Get sign-off, hand over, and close | acceptance records, handover confirmation, closure record, remaining conditions if any | Accept, conditionally accept, reject, or close | acceptance and closure basis are explicit |

## Minimum Weekly Operating Rhythm

Update at least:

- status by deliverable and evidence status, not activity alone
- RAID and dependency view
- decision and change log
- acceptance tracker showing what is ready, blocked, or awaiting sign-off

## Red Flags

Stop and correct course if you see:

- delivery work starting before Stage 3 authorization
- Stage 2 turning into detailed design
- Stage 4 adding scope that was not authorized
- missing Acceptance Authority for an in-scope deliverable or domain
- evidence being reconstructed late instead of linked as work completes
- status reporting that shows activity but not deliverable readiness
- work described as done even though acceptance is not explicit

## Required Checks

Confirm:

- the current stage matches the work that is actually happening
- the work record still reflects the approved intent
- decisions and changes are attributable to the right authority
- acceptance expectations are visible before the end of delivery
- closure is not claimed without acceptance or explicit conditions
