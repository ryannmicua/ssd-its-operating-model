# Compliance and Regulatory Alignment Statement Specification

## 1. Purpose

States how the initiative aligns with applicable legal, regulatory, policy, and internal control requirements.

## 2. Governance Boundary

This specification defines what the deliverable must contain and how it supports acceptance.

It does not replace approval authority, stage-gate decisions, or other governance artifacts.

## 3. When It Is Required

This deliverable is required when its domain is in scope in the Standard Deliverables Reference.

## 4. Intended Readers and Users

- Deliverable author(s)
- Delivery Owner
- Domain Acceptance Authority
- Reviewers and maintainers who depend on the deliverable

## 5. Minimum Required Content

The deliverable must include:

- Clear statement of scope and intent
- Named owner responsible for content accuracy
- Key decisions, assumptions, and constraints
- Dependencies and risks relevant to the deliverable
- Traceability references to related deliverables where applicable
- Version/date metadata and change summary

## 6. Quality and Acceptance Checks

The deliverable is fit for acceptance when:

- Content is clear, practical, and understandable by non-specialists
- Ownership and decision points are explicit
- Scope boundaries are clear and not contradictory to approved scope
- Required sections are complete and reviewable
- Acceptance Authority can make a defensible acceptance decision

## 7. Relationship to Other Deliverables

This deliverable must stay aligned with the Initiative Definition and with other in-scope domain deliverables.

Where overlap exists, summarize once and reference the source artifact instead of duplicating content.

## 8. Maintenance Expectations

Update the deliverable when material scope, risk, ownership, or operating assumptions change.

Keep historical versions or change logs so decisions remain auditable.
