# Change & Communication Plan Specification

## 1. Purpose and Intended Outcome

The Change & Communication Plan defines how the initiative will be explained, promoted, reinforced, and managed across affected stakeholders and user groups.

It exists to make communications intentional, audience-aware, and linked to readiness rather than treated as an afterthought. A useful plan shows who needs to hear what, when they need to hear it, who will communicate it, and how feedback or escalation will be handled.

The intended outcome is that the right audiences receive the right messages at the right time to support awareness, readiness, reinforcement, and adoption.

## 2. When It Is Required

This artifact is required when user behavior, stakeholder expectations, process adoption, or organizational awareness materially affects initiative success.

## 3. Intended Readers and Users

- Change or Communications Lead
- Business Owner
- Delivery Owner
- leaders sponsoring the change
- training and adoption support teams

## 4. Intended Project Context

Use this artifact after user impacts are understood and before launch or rollout activities begin. It is most useful where different audiences need different messages, channels, timing, or sponsorship.

It should align with PMI communications and stakeholder-engagement practice by making audience, message, timing, and sender explicit. It should also align with ITIL organizational change management by supporting adoption through deliberate communication rather than assumption.

In the Work Delivery Framework lifecycle, this artifact is usually elaborated in Stage 4, activated during Stage 5 mobilization, and executed and maintained through Stage 6 delivery and rollout.

## 5. How Much Detail to Include

Include enough detail to show audience, message, timing, channel, sender, and feedback path. Do not turn it into a copy repository for every message or campaign asset.

## 6. Required Content or Minimum Structure

### 6.1. Plan context

Must include:

- initiative or rollout name
- communication objective
- date or version
- owner

This section identifies what change the plan is supporting.

### 6.2. Required content for each communication row

Each communication row must include:

- audience group
- key message
- timing or milestone
- channel
- sender or owner
- feedback or escalation path
- intended communication outcome where clarity, action, or reassurance differs by audience

Recommended columns:

| Audience | Key message | Timing | Channel | Sender | Intended outcome | Feedback or escalation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

This row structure keeps the plan audience-specific and actionable.

### 6.3. Leadership and reinforcement expectations

Should include:

- where leadership alignment is needed
- who is expected to reinforce the change
- any high-risk audience groups needing extra communication attention

This section helps the plan support adoption, not just announcement.

### 6.4. Promotional or awareness materials note

May include:

- awareness pages, launch materials, or campaign content created only to support adoption

If a website or portal delivers core business capability rather than communications support, it belongs in Solution Deliverables instead.

## 7. What to Keep Out

Keep the following out of this artifact:

- full training content
- detailed issue logs
- core business functionality requirements for websites or portals that belong in Solution Deliverables

## 9. Ownership, Review, and Acceptance Expectations

The Change or Communications Lead usually owns this artifact with Business Owner input.

Review should include sponsors or leaders expected to send or reinforce key messages.

The Delivery Owner is accountable for ensuring communication actions are integrated into mobilization and delivery control rhythm. The Business Owner, as the usual Acceptance Authority for adoption outcomes, should confirm that critical communication commitments are met before closure.

## 10. Maintenance Expectations

Update when rollout timing, impacted audiences, message content, or feedback channels change materially.

## 11. Validation Guide

- Are the right audiences identified with meaningful messages?
- Is the timing and delivery approach practical?
- Is the intended outcome of each communication clear enough to judge whether the message actually did its job?
- Are leadership expectations and feedback paths explicit?
- Does the plan stay focused on change communications rather than turning into a training pack or web requirements document?

If weak, improve audience segmentation and make communication ownership clearer.

## 12. Prompt Guide for Drafting the Artifact

### 12.1. Starter prompt

> Draft a Change & Communication Plan for this initiative.
> Define the audience groups, key messages, communication timing, channels, senders, leadership expectations, and feedback or escalation paths needed to support adoption.
> Keep it audience-specific and practical.
