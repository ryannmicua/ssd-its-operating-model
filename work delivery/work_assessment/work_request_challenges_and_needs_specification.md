# Work Request Challenges and Needs Specification

## 1. Purpose and Intended Outcome

The Work Request Challenges and Needs artifact captures the business challenges, pain points, unmet needs, and triggering conditions behind a work request.

It exists to improve problem framing early in Work Assessment when the request arrives as a partial idea, a solution-shaped ask, or a bundle of symptoms without a clear statement of need. A useful Work Request Challenges and Needs artifact is lightweight, practical, and explicit about who is affected, what is happening today, why it matters now, and what consequence follows if the issue is left unresolved.

The intended outcome is that the work request has a clearer problem basis before deeper assessment begins and later analysis does not need to reconstruct the basic business need from scratch.

## 2. When It Is Required

This artifact is optional within Work Assessment.

Use it when the initial request does not yet express the underlying need clearly enough for a sound Initial Review or Validation Assessment, especially where:

- the request is phrased mainly as a solution idea
- multiple pain points are blended together
- the affected parties or business impact are still unclear
- the urgency is asserted but not yet explained

## 3. Intended Readers and Users

- Assessment Owner
- requestor or submitter
- requester subject matter experts and immediate team
- sponsor or sponsor candidate
- ITS intake or governance reviewers
- later assessment or definition practitioners who need the original problem basis

## 4. Intended Project Context

Use this artifact at the front of Work Assessment or early in Validation Assessment when the team needs a clearer statement of the current problem basis before deciding how much further analysis is justified.

It is not a business case and not a requirements document. It is a lightweight assessment artifact used to sharpen the problem statement and visible need.

## 5. How Much Detail to Include

Keep it light but specific.

Include enough detail to make the business challenge understandable to someone who was not part of the original request conversation.

At minimum, the artifact should make visible:

- who is affected
- what is happening today
- where the issue appears
- why it matters now
- likely causes or contributing factors where visible
- the consequence of inaction
- the difference between symptoms and underlying needs where that distinction can already be seen

The governing principle is:

> Frame the problem clearly enough to support assessment decisions, but stop before turning business pain into detailed requirements or premature solution choices.

## 6. Required Content or Minimum Structure

### 6.1. Request identity and source

Must include:

- work request or issue title
- requestor or source
- Assessment Owner
- date identified
- short note on what triggered the request now

### 6.2. Affected parties

Must include:

- who is most affected by the issue
- which business units, service areas, user groups, or operational roles are materially affected

### 6.3. Current issue description

Must include:

- what is happening today
- where the issue appears in practice
- how the issue is typically experienced by the affected parties

### 6.4. Business challenge or pain point summary

Must include:

- the main pain points, obstacles, delays, errors, or unmet expectations visible now
- why those conditions are a problem for the business, service, user, or control environment

### 6.5. Trigger and timing

Must include:

- why the issue matters now rather than later
- any event, change, deadline, growth pattern, incident, or operational condition that brought the issue forward

### 6.6. Likely causes or contributing factors

Should include where visible:

- the apparent causes or contributing conditions
- any uncertainty about root causes that still needs validation

Do not force false certainty where the causes are still unclear.

### 6.7. Impact and consequence of inaction

Must include:

- the operational, business, service, control, or user impact visible today
- what is likely to happen if nothing changes
- why the consequence of inaction matters enough to record

### 6.8. Symptoms versus underlying needs

Must include:

- the visible symptoms or complaints
- the underlying need or needs that appear to sit behind them where that can already be seen
- any note where the underlying need still needs validation during later assessment

### 6.9. Assessment-use summary

Must include:

- the concise problem basis that should carry into Initial Review or Validation Assessment
- any question that the next assessment step must test or clarify
- explicit note of any solution assumptions that should not be treated as accepted need

## 7. What to Keep Out

Keep the following out of this artifact:

- detailed requirements
- solution design
- product or vendor selection
- implementation planning
- detailed option analysis
- benefits cases built on untested assumptions

## 8. Relationships to Other Artifacts

This artifact can be used before or alongside the [Initial Review](initial_review_specification.md) when the original request needs clearer problem framing.

It may also inform the [Validation Assessment](validation_assessment_specification.md) and the [Current State Analysis Report](current_state_analysis_report_specification.md) where deeper assessment proceeds.

## 9. Ownership, Review, and Acceptance Expectations

The Assessment Owner usually prepares this artifact with the requestor and the requester's subject matter experts.

Review it with the people closest to the issue so the documented challenge reflects what is happening today and not only what the request was first assumed to mean.

## 10. Maintenance Expectations

This artifact is usually stable once the problem basis has been accepted into later assessment.

Update it if the understanding of who is affected, what the issue is, or why it matters changes materially during Validation Assessment.

## 11. Validation Guide

- Does the artifact make the current challenge understandable without naming a solution as if it were the need?
- Are the affected parties, current issue, business context, timing, impacts, and consequence of inaction visible enough to support assessment?
- Does it distinguish symptoms from underlying needs where possible?
- Are likely causes or contributing factors included only where they are visible, without forcing false certainty?
- Does it stay lightweight and avoid behaving like a requirement set, business case, or design brief?

## 12. Prompt Guide for Drafting the Artifact

### 12.1. Starter prompt

> Draft a Work Request Challenges and Needs summary for this work request.
> Explain who is affected, what is happening today, where the issue appears, why it matters now, what challenges or unmet needs are visible, what likely causes or contributing factors can already be seen, what impact is occurring, what happens if nothing changes, and which symptoms should be distinguished from the underlying need.
> Keep it lightweight, practical, and free of premature solutioning.

### 12.2. Section prompts

> Rewrite the issue description so it explains the current business problem and affected parties without assuming that the requested solution is already the right answer.

> Separate the visible symptoms from the underlying need and identify what still needs validation in later assessment.

### 12.3. Validation prompts

> Check whether this artifact gives Work Assessment a clear problem basis without drifting into requirements, design, or business-case language.

> Rewrite any section that reads like a preferred solution pitch rather than a problem-framing artifact.
